"Deep-fried water" is a bizarre food trend where a globule of water is encased in an edible, jelly-like membrane created using a chemical process called spherification, making it safe to handle and coat for frying. If this membrane is broken during frying, the rapid expansion of water into steam (about 1,800 times its volume) can cause a dangerous explosion of scalding oil. The resulting fried water doesn't taste like anything in particular, except the taste of the batter (usually flour, egg, and breadcrumbs) and the frying oil.

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of stacked layers of wide, flat pasta sheets. These layers are alternated with fillings such as ragù (meat sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, and various cheeses including ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. The assembled dish is then typically topped with more cheese and baked in an oven until bubbly and golden.
